Lucie Rie (1902-1995) was born in Vienna, Austria to a wealthy Jewish family. She went on to study pottery under the Austrian potter and sculptor, Michael Powolny at the Vienna Kunstgewerbeschule. The school was associated with the Wiener Werkstatte and the arts and crafts movement. After opening her own studio in Vienna in 1925, Lucie Rie exhibited her work that same year in Paris. Notably, in 1937 she won a silver medal at the Paris International Exhibition. In 1938, as the Nazis occupied Austria, Lucie Rie fled to London, England. She remained there throughout her life. Lucie Rie is best known for her functional ceramics, primarily bowls, bottles and vases. Her modernist pottery appears rustic and organic and features a wide array of colours and textures. Lucie Rie's work exudes textures and shapes reminiscient of both the naturual world and Japanese influence. Although delicate and artistic, Lucie Rie encouraged practical use of her pottery.
